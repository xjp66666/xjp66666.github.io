# Why Hexo

A few years ago, when I was in elementary school, I build my first web blog.

The reason was because one day when my math teacher was chanting lullaby as usual and I was whispering to my desk mate in order to not fall asleep. I can't remember why I said that but "I wish I could have my own website. That would be sooo cool".

Coincidentally he is a computer tech master. Yes. When everyone was forced to study 15 hours a day, he still managed to take some time to learn Coding.

"Why not using Hexo?" he said, "its neat, easy-to-use, and highly customizable. You don't even have to write a single line of html code cuz Hexo is capable of generating static web pages from your markdown files."

# First try

Back then I had no idea what he was talking about and what he meant by "run hexo on github ci and deploy your website on github pages". So I search for tutorials after getting home and spent the whole night and finally figured out that html and markdown are not programming languages. They are actually mark up languages.

I started building it in the summer vacation that came after my grade 7 school year. It took me almost two weeks. Days and nights.

At that time(2022) people still thought that "AI" is some kind of advanced tech and it would only exist in sci-fis, although ChatGPT went viral not long after the beginning of 2023. So when I ran into problems, the best I could do was googling. I assume when you're reading this article, most people have already given up using search engines, but that's for later.

And when I finally finished my masterpiece, I couldn't describe how thrilled I was cuz it was the first time I literally built something I was proud of, all on my own.

I posted it online.

I showed it off to my classmates, my parents, my teachers, although it seemed nobody really cared.

I also bought a .site domain for it()

I was already tired of those compliments from others, so nothing could let me down even if no one else saw my work.

Well, maybe except for my schoolwork.
